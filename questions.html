GRAVITATION 

1) QUESTION: If the mass of the Earth were to double while its radius remains the same, what would happen to the acceleration due to gravity on the surface of the Earth?

A) It would remain same

B) It would triple

C) It would double

D) It would quadruple

ANSWER: Option C: It would double

EXPLANATION: The acceleration due to gravity on the surface of a planet is given by the formula  g=r2GM​ where G is the gravitational constant, M is the mass of the planet, and r is the radius of the planet1. If we were to double the mass of the Earth while keeping its radius constant, we would effectively double the acceleration due to gravity on its surface1. This is because g is directly proportional to M in the formula1. Therefore, if M doubles, g would also double
