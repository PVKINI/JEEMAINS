Elastic behaviour,

Sure, here’s a challenging question on “Elastic Behavior” for your JEE preparation:

Question: A spring of spring constant k is stretched by a force F causing an extension x. If the force applied is doubled, what will be the new extension in the spring?

Options:

1.     x

2.     2x

3.     4x

4.     x/2

Answer: The correct answer is Option 2: 2x.

Explanation: According to Hooke’s Law, the force F required to stretch or compress a spring by a distance x from its natural length is proportional to x, and is given by the formula F = kx, where k is the spring constant.

If the force applied is doubled, F becomes 2F. Substituting this into the formula, we get 2F = kx', where x' is the new extension.

Comparing this with the original formula, we find that x' = 2x.

Hence, the new extension in the spring is 2x.

 

Stress-strain relationship,

Sure, here’s a challenging question on “Stress-Strain Relationship” for your JEE preparation:

Question: A wire of length L and cross-sectional area A is subjected to a force F that causes it to extend by ΔL. The material of the wire obeys Hooke’s Law. If the force applied is doubled, what will be the new extension in the wire?

Options:

1.     ΔL

2.     2ΔL

3.     4ΔL

4.     ΔL/2

Answer: The correct answer is Option 2: 2ΔL.

Explanation: According to Hooke’s Law, the stress σ in a material is directly proportional to the strain ε. Stress is defined as the force F divided by the cross-sectional area A, and strain is defined as the extension ΔL divided by the original length L. So, we have σ = F/A = kε = k(ΔL/L), where k is the proportionality constant.

If the force applied is doubled, F becomes 2F. Substituting this into the formula, we get σ' = 2F/A = kε' = k(ΔL'/L), where ΔL' is the new extension.

Comparing this with the original formula, we find that ΔL' = 2ΔL.

Hence, the new extension in the wire is 2ΔL.

 

Hooke’s Law.

Sure, here’s a challenging question on “Hooke’s Law” for your JEE preparation:

Question: A spring of spring constant k is stretched by a force F causing an extension x. If the spring constant is doubled, what will be the new extension in the spring for the same force applied?

Options:

1.     x

2.     2x

3.     x/2

4.     4x

Answer: The correct answer is Option 3: x/2.

Explanation: According to Hooke’s Law, the force F required to stretch or compress a spring by a distance x from its natural length is proportional to x, and is given by the formula F = kx, where k is the spring constant.

If the spring constant is doubled, k becomes 2k. Substituting this into the formula, we get F = 2kx', where x' is the new extension.

Comparing this with the original formula, we find that x' = x/2.

Hence, the new extension in the spring is x/2.

 

Young’s modulus,

Sure, here’s a challenging question on “Young’s Modulus” for your JEE preparation:

Question: A wire of length L and cross-sectional area A is made of a material with Young’s modulus Y. It is subjected to a tensile stress that causes an extension ΔL. If the length of the wire is doubled while keeping the applied force and the material of the wire constant, what will be the new extension in the wire?

Options:

1.     ΔL

2.     2ΔL

3.     ΔL/2

4.     4ΔL

Answer: The correct answer is Option 1: ΔL.

Explanation: According to the definition of Young’s modulus, Y = Stress/Strain = (F/A) / (ΔL/L), where F is the applied force.

If the length of the wire is doubled, L becomes 2L. However, since the applied force and the material of the wire are kept constant, the stress in the wire remains the same. Therefore, the strain in the wire (which is equal to Stress/Y) also remains the same.

Since strain is defined as the extension divided by the original length, and both the strain and original length have doubled, it follows that the extension ΔL' must remain constant.

Hence, the new extension in the wire is ΔL.

 

bulk modulus,

Sure, here’s a challenging question on “Bulk Modulus” for your JEE preparation:

Question: A solid cube of side a and volume V is subjected to a uniform pressure P that causes a decrease in its volume ΔV. The material of the cube has a bulk modulus B. If the pressure applied is doubled, what will be the new change in volume?

Options:

1.     ΔV

2.     2ΔV

3.     ΔV/2

4.     4ΔV

Answer: The correct answer is Option 2: 2ΔV.

Explanation: According to the definition of bulk modulus, B = P/(ΔV/V), where P is the applied pressure.

If the pressure applied is doubled, P becomes 2P. Substituting this into the formula, we get B = 2P/(ΔV'/V), where ΔV' is the new change in volume.

Comparing this with the original formula, we find that ΔV' = 2ΔV.

Hence, the new change in volume is 2ΔV.

 

modulus of rigidity.

Sure, here’s a challenging question on “Modulus of Rigidity” for your JEE preparation:

Question: A solid cylinder of radius r and length L is subjected to a shearing force F that causes a lateral displacement x in its top surface. The material of the cylinder has a modulus of rigidity η. If the shearing force applied is doubled, what will be the new lateral displacement?

Options:

1.     x

2.     2x

3.     x/2

4.     4x

Answer: The correct answer is Option 2: 2x.

Explanation: According to the definition of modulus of rigidity, η = F/(A*x), where F is the applied shearing force, A is the area over which the force is applied (which is equal to πr² for a cylinder), and x is the lateral displacement.

If the shearing force applied is doubled, F becomes 2F. Substituting this into the formula, we get η = 2F/(A*x'), where x' is the new lateral displacement.

Comparing this with the original formula, we find that x' = 2x.

Hence, the new lateral displacement is 2x.

 

Pressure due to a fluid column;

Sure, here’s a challenging question on “Pressure Due to Fluid Column” for your JEE preparation:

Question: A fluid column of height h and density ρ exerts a pressure P at its base. The acceleration due to gravity is g. If the height of the fluid column is doubled and its density is halved, what will be the new pressure at the base of the fluid column?

Options:

1.     P

2.     2P

3.     P/2

4.     4P

Answer: The correct answer is Option 1: P.

Explanation: The pressure P at the base of a fluid column is given by the formula P = ρgh, where ρ is the density of the fluid, g is the acceleration due to gravity, and h is the height of the fluid column.

If the height of the fluid column is doubled, h becomes 2h. If the density of the fluid is halved, ρ becomes ρ/2.

Substituting these values into the formula, we get P' = (ρ/2)g(2h) = ρgh = P.

Hence, the new pressure at the base of the fluid column is P.
