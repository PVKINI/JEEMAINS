REFERENCE FRAME

Question: A train is moving with a velocity of 20 m/s towards east. A bird is flying with a velocity of 10 m/s in the north direction with respect to the train. What is the velocity of the bird with respect to an observer standing on the ground?

Options:

1.       10 m/s, North

2.       20 m/s, East

3.       22.36 m/s, North-East

4.       30 m/s, North-East

The correct answer is option 3. The bird’s velocity with respect to the ground can be found using vector addition since the velocities are at right angles to each other. The magnitude of the resultant velocity is √((20)^2 + (10)^2) = 22.36 m/s and the direction is North-East.

 

Question: A car travels 100 km to the east, then 100 km to the west. The total time for the journey is 4 hours. What are the average speed and average velocity of the car?

Options:

1.       Speed = 50 km/h, Velocity = 0 km/h

2.       Speed = 50 km/h, Velocity = 50 km/h

3.       Speed = 0 km/h, Velocity = 0 km/h

4.       Speed = 100 km/h, Velocity = 100 km/h

Answer: Option 1.

Explanation: The total distance travelled by the car is 200 km (100 km east + 100 km west), and the time taken is 4 hours. So, the average speed is total distance divided by total time, which is 200 km / 4 h = 50 km/h.

However, because velocity is a vector quantity that takes into account direction, the displacement of the car (final position - initial position) is zero (since it ended up in the same place it started). Therefore, the average velocity is total displacement divided by total time, which is 0 km / 4 h = 0 km/h.

 

Question: A particle moves along a straight line such that its displacement ‘s’ at any time ‘t’ is given by the equation s = t^3 - 6t^2 + 9t + 5. What is the velocity of the particle when t = 2 seconds?

Options:

1.       2 m/s

2.       -3 m/s

3.       0 m/s

4.       5 m/s

Answer: Option 2.

Explanation: The velocity of the particle is given by the derivative of the displacement with respect to time. So, v = ds/dt = 3t^2 - 12t + 9. Substituting t = 2 seconds into this equation gives v = 3*(2)^2 - 12*2 + 9 = -3 m/s. Therefore, the velocity of the particle at t = 2 seconds is -3 m/s.

 

MOTION IN A STRAIGHT LINE

Question: A car starts from rest and accelerates uniformly to reach a speed of 21 m/s in 7 seconds. It then continues at this constant speed for 300 meters before applying brakes and stopping uniformly in 10 seconds. What is the total distance covered by the car?

Options:

1.       525 meters

2.       600 meters

3.       675 meters

4.       750 meters

Answer: Option 3.

Explanation: The total distance covered by the car is the sum of the distances covered in each phase of its journey.

1.       During the acceleration phase, the car covers a distance given by the equation of motion s = ut + 0.5at^2, where u is the initial velocity (0 m/s), a is the acceleration (21 m/s / 7 s = 3 m/s^2), and t is the time (7 s). Substituting these values gives s = 07 + 0.53*(7)^2 = 73.5 meters.

2.       The car then travels at a constant speed of 21 m/s for 300 meters, so this phase contributes 300 meters to the total distance.

3.       Finally, during deceleration, the car again covers a distance given by s = ut - 0.5at^2, where u is now the initial velocity (21 m/s), a is the deceleration (21 m/s / 10 s = -2.1 m/s^2), and t is the time (10 s). Substituting these values gives s = 2110 - 0.5(-2.1)*(10)^2 = 301.5 meters.

Adding up these distances gives a total distance of 73.5 + 300 + 301.5 = 675 meters.

Question: A particle moves in a straight line such that its displacement ‘s’ from the origin at any time ‘t’ is given by the equation s = t^3 - 3t^2 + 2t + 1. What is the speed of the particle at t = 2 seconds?

Options:

1.       2 m/s

2.       4 m/s

3.       6 m/s

4.       8 m/s

Answer: Option 3.

Explanation: The velocity of the particle is given by the derivative of the displacement with respect to time. So, v = ds/dt = 3t^2 - 6t + 2. Substituting t = 2 seconds into this equation gives v = 3*(2)^2 - 6*2 + 2 = -2 m/s. However, speed is the magnitude of velocity and is always positive, so the speed of the particle at t = 2 seconds is |-2 m/s| = 2 m/s.

 

Uniform and non-uniform motion

Question: A car travels first half of the distance between two points with a speed of v1 = 30 km/hr and the second half with a speed of v2 = 50 km/hr. What is the average speed of the car for the entire journey?

Options:

1.       37.5 km/hr

2.       40 km/hr

3.       42.5 km/hr

4.       45 km/hr

Answer: Option 2.

Explanation: The average speed for the entire journey is not simply the average of the two speeds, because the time spent at each speed is different. The average speed is given by the total distance divided by the total time. Since the distances traveled at each speed are equal, we can say that the total distance is 2d (where d is either half of the distance). The time to travel each half of the distance is d/v1 and d/v2 respectively. Therefore, the average speed v_avg = 2d / (d/v1 + d/v2) = 2 / (1/v1 + 1/v2). Substituting v1 = 30 km/hr and v2 = 50 km/hr into this equation gives v_avg = 2 / (1/30 + 1/50) = 40 km/hr.

 

AVERAGE SPEED

Question: A car travels from point A to point B at a speed of 40 km/hr and returns from point B to point A at a speed of 60 km/hr. What is the average speed of the car for the entire journey?

Options:

1.       48 km/hr

2.       50 km/hr

3.       52 km/hr

4.       54 km/hr

Answer: Option 1.

Explanation: The average speed for the entire journey is not simply the average of the two speeds, because the time spent at each speed is different. The average speed is given by the total distance divided by the total time. Let d be the distance between points A and B. The time to travel from A to B is d/40 and the time to travel from B to A is d/60. Therefore, the average speed v_avg = 2d / (d/40 + d/60) = 2 / (1/40 + 1/60) = 48 km/hr.

Uniformly accelerated motion

Question: A car starts from rest and accelerates uniformly to reach a speed of 72 km/hr in 10 seconds. It then continues at this constant speed for 200 meters before applying brakes and stopping uniformly in 5 seconds. What is the total distance covered by the car?

Options:

1.       225 meters

2.       250 meters

3.       275 meters

4.       300 meters

Answer: Option 2.

Explanation: The total distance covered by the car is the sum of the distances covered in each phase of its journey.

1.       During the acceleration phase, the car covers a distance given by the equation of motion s = ut + 0.5at^2, where u is the initial velocity (0 m/s), a is the acceleration (72 km/hr / 10 s = 2 m/s^2), and t is the time (10 s). Substituting these values gives s = 010 + 0.52*(10)^2 = 100 meters.

2.       The car then travels at a constant speed of 72 km/hr for 200 meters, so this phase contributes 200 meters to the total distance.

3.       Finally, during deceleration, the car again covers a distance given by s = ut - 0.5at^2, where u is now the initial velocity (72 km/hr), a is the deceleration (72 km/hr / 5 s = -14.4 m/s^2), and t is the time (5 s). Substituting these values gives s = (72 km/hr)5 - 0.5(-14.4 m/s2)*(5)2 = -50 meters.

VELOCITY & TIME

Question: A particle moves along a straight line with an initial velocity of 10 m/s. Its acceleration is given by the equation a = 2t, where t is the time in seconds. What is the velocity of the particle at t = 5 seconds?

Options:

1.       20 m/s

2.       30 m/s

3.       40 m/s

4.       50 m/s

Answer: Option 4.

Explanation: The velocity of the particle at any time t can be found by integrating the acceleration with respect to time. The indefinite integral of a = 2t is v = t^2 + C, where C is the constant of integration. Since the initial velocity of the particle is 10 m/s, we can find C by setting t = 0 and v = 10 in the equation v = t^2 + C, which gives C = 10. Therefore, the equation for the velocity of the particle is v = t^2 + 10. Substituting t = 5 seconds into this equation gives v = (5)^2 + 10 = 50 m/s.

 

Position-time graph

Question: The velocity-time graph of a particle moving along a straight line is a straight line passing through the origin and making an angle of 45° with the time axis. The initial velocity of the particle is zero. What is the acceleration of the particle?

Options:

1.      0 m/s²

2.      1 m/s²

3.      2 m/s²

4.      4 m/s²

Answer: Option 2.

Explanation: In a velocity-time graph, the acceleration of the particle is given by the slope of the graph. Since the graph is a straight line passing through the origin and making an angle of 45° with the time axis, the slope of the line is tan(45°) = 1. Therefore, the acceleration of the particle is 1 m/s².

 

Question: The position-time graph of a particle moving along a straight line is a parabola with its vertex at the origin. The initial position of the particle is zero. What is the nature of the motion of the particle?

Options:

1.       Uniform motion

2.       Non-uniform motion with constant acceleration

3.       Non-uniform motion with non-constant acceleration

4.       None of the above

Answer: Option 3.

Explanation: In a position-time graph, the slope of the tangent to the graph at any point represents the velocity of the particle at that instant. Since the graph is a parabola, its slope (and hence the velocity of the particle) is changing continuously. This means that the particle is undergoing non-uniform motion.

Furthermore, the acceleration of the particle is given by the rate of change of its velocity. Since the velocity is changing continuously, the acceleration must also be changing continuously. Therefore, the particle is undergoing non-uniform motion with non-constant acceleration.

Scalars and Vectors

Question: Consider two vectors A and B such that |A| = 3, |B| = 4, and the angle between A and B is 60 degrees. What is the magnitude of the resultant vector R = A + B?

Options:

1.       5 units

2.       6 units

3.       7 units

4.       8 units

Answer: Option 3.

Explanation: The magnitude of the resultant vector R = A + B can be found using the formula |R| = sqrt(|A|^2 + |B|^2 + 2|A||B|cosθ), where θ is the angle between A and B. Substituting |A| = 3, |B| = 4, and cos(60°) = 0.5 into this equation gives |R| = sqrt((3)^2 + (4)^2 + 234*0.5) = sqrt(9 + 16 + 12) = sqrt(37) ≈ 7 units.

 

Vector. Addition and subtraction

Question: Consider two vectors A and B such that |A| = 5 units, |B| = 10 units, and the angle between A and B is 60 degrees. What is the magnitude of the resultant vector R = A - B?

Options:

1.       5 units

2.       10 units

3.       15 units

4.       20 units

Answer: Option 1.

Explanation: The magnitude of the resultant vector R = A - B can be found using the formula |R| = sqrt(|A|^2 + |B|^2 - 2|A||B|cosθ), where θ is the angle between A and B. Substituting |A| = 5 units, |B| = 10 units, and cos(60°) = 0.5 into this equation gives |R| = sqrt((5)^2 + (10)^2 - 2510*0.5) = sqrt(25 + 100 - 50) = sqrt(75) ≈ 5 units.

Scalar and vector products

Question: Consider two vectors A and B such that |A| = 3 units, |B| = 4 units, and the angle between A and B is 60 degrees. What are the magnitudes of the scalar product (dot product) and vector product (cross product) of A and B?

Options:

1.       Scalar Product = 6 units, Vector Product = 12 units

2.       Scalar Product = 12 units, Vector Product = 6 units

3.       Scalar Product = 6 units, Vector Product = 6 units

4.       Scalar Product = 12 units, Vector Product = 12 units

Answer: Option 1.

Explanation: The scalar product (dot product) of two vectors A and B is given by A.B = |A||B|cosθ, where θ is the angle between A and B. Substituting |A| = 3 units, |B| = 4 units, and cos(60°) = 0.5 into this equation gives A.B = 340.5 = 6 units.

The vector product (cross product) of two vectors A and B is given by |AxB| = |A||B|sinθ. Substituting |A| = 3 units, |B| = 4 units, and sin(60°) = sqrt(3)/2 into this equation gives |AxB| = 34sqrt(3)/2 ≈ 12 units.

 

 Unit Vector,

Question: A unit vector u is rotated about the x-axis by an angle of 90 degrees to obtain a new unit vector v. This vector v is then rotated about the original unit vector u by an angle of 90 degrees to obtain a third unit vector w. What is the angle between the original unit vector u and the final unit vector w?

Options:

1.       0 degrees

2.       45 degrees

3.       90 degrees

4.       180 degrees

Answer: The correct answer is 3. 90 degrees.

Explanation: When the unit vector u is rotated about the x-axis by 90 degrees, it moves into the y-z plane, forming vector v. When v is then rotated about u, it moves in a plane perpendicular to u, forming vector w. Therefore, the angle between vectors u and w is 90 degrees.

 

Resolution of a Vector.

Question: A vector A of magnitude 10 units is inclined at an angle of 60 degrees with the x-axis in the x-y plane. The vector is then rotated about the y-axis by an angle of 45 degrees. What are the x and z components of the resulting vector?

Options:

1.      Ax = 5 units, Az = 5√3 units

2.      Ax = 5√3 units, Az = 5 units

3.      Ax = 5√2 units, Az = 5√2 units

4.      Ax = 10 units, Az = 0 units

Answer: The correct answer is 1. Ax = 5 units, Az = 5√3 units.

Explanation: Initially, the x-component of vector A is Acos(60) = 10cos(60) = 5 units and there is no z-component. When the vector is rotated about the y-axis by 45 degrees, the x-component is divided equally between the x and z directions. Therefore, the x and z components of the resulting vector are both 5 units.

Question: Two cars, A and B, are moving in the same direction along a straight road. Car A is moving with a constant speed of 60 km/h and car B is moving with a speed of 80 km/h. At a certain point on the road, car B is 100 km behind car A. How long will it take for car B to overtake car A?

Options:

1.       1 hour

2.       2 hours

3.       3 hours

4.       4 hours

Answer: The correct answer is 2. 2 hours.

Explanation: The relative speed of car B with respect to car A is the difference in their speeds, which is 80 km/h - 60 km/h = 20 km/h. The time it takes for car B to overtake car A is the distance between them divided by the relative speed, which is 100 km / 20 km/h = 5 hours.

 

Motion in a plane,

Question: A particle is projected from the ground with an initial speed of 20 m/s at an angle of 30 degrees to the horizontal. At the highest point of its trajectory, it explodes into two fragments of equal mass. One fragment, whose speed immediately after the explosion is zero, falls vertically down. How far from the point of projection does the other fragment land?

Options:

1.       20 m

2.       40 m

3.       60 m

4.       80 m

Answer: The correct answer is 3. 60 m.

Explanation: The horizontal distance covered by the particle before explosion can be calculated using the equation of motion in a plane. The time to reach the highest point is given by (usinθ)/g, where u is the initial speed, θ is the angle of projection, and g is the acceleration due to gravity. The horizontal distance is then ucosθt, which gives 20cos(30)20/(29.8) = 20 m.

After the explosion, one fragment falls vertically down and the other continues with the same horizontal velocity as before (since no external horizontal force is acting on it). Therefore, it covers an additional horizontal distance during its fall under gravity, which can be calculated as ucosθt = 20*cos(30)*20/9.8 = 40 m.

So, the total horizontal distance covered by this fragment from the point of projection is 20 m + 40 m = 60 m.

 

Projectile Motion,

Question: A projectile is launched from the ground with an initial speed of 50 m/s at an angle of 45 degrees to the horizontal. At what time will the projectile be at a height of 60 m above the ground?

Options:

1.       1 second

2.       2 seconds

3.       3 seconds

4.       4 seconds

Answer: The correct answer is 3. 3 seconds.

Explanation: The vertical component of the initial velocity is given by usinθ, where u is the initial speed and θ is the angle of projection. So, it is 50sin(45) = 50/√2 m/s.

The time it takes for the projectile to reach a height of 60 m can be calculated using the equation of motion: h = ut - 0.5gt², where h is the height, u is the initial vertical velocity, g is the acceleration due to gravity, and t is time. Solving this equation for t gives t = (60*2)/(50/√2) = √(24/√2) ≈ 3 seconds.

 

Uniform Circular Motion.

Question: A particle is moving in a circular path of radius 1 m with a constant speed of 2 m/s. If the mass of the particle is 1 kg, what is the net force acting on the particle?

Options:

1.       0 N

2.       1 N

3.       2 N

4.       4 N

Answer: The correct answer is 4. 4 N.

Explanation: In uniform circular motion, the net force acting on the particle is the centripetal force, which is given by F = m*v²/r, where m is the mass of the particle, v is the speed of the particle, and r is the radius of the circular path. Substituting the given values, we get F = 1 kg * (2 m/s)² / 1 m = 4 N.

 

 Question: A car is moving in a circular path of radius 100 m with a constant speed of 20 m/s. What is the magnitude of the acceleration of the car?

Options:

1.       0 m/s²

2.       2 m/s²

3.       4 m/s²

4.       8 m/s²

Answer: The correct answer is 4. 8 m/s².

Explanation: In uniform circular motion, the acceleration of the particle is the centripetal acceleration, which is given by a = v²/r, where v is the speed of the particle and r is the radius of the circular path. Substituting the given values, we get a = (20 m/s)² / 100 m = 400/100 = 4 m/s².

 

Question: A stone is tied to a string and whirled in a horizontal circle of radius 0.5 m. The string makes an angle of 30 degrees with the vertical. If the gravitational acceleration is 10 m/s², what is the speed of the stone?

Options:

1.       1 m/s

2.       √3 m/s

3.       2 m/s

4.       2√3 m/s

Answer: The correct answer is 2. √3 m/s.

Explanation: In uniform circular motion, the tension in the string provides the necessary centripetal force for the stone to move in a circle. The vertical component of the tension balances the weight of the stone, and the horizontal component provides the centripetal force. From these conditions, we can derive the speed of the stone as v = √(grtanθ), where g is the gravitational acceleration, r is the radius of the circle, and θ is the angle between the string and the vertical. Substituting the given values, we get v = √(100.5tan(30)) = √3 m/s.
