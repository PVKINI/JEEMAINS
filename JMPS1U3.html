Question: A block of mass 2 kg is placed on a frictionless inclined plane of inclination 30 degrees. The block is connected to a spring of spring constant 100 N/m, with the spring parallel to the incline. The block is displaced slightly and starts oscillating. What is the frequency of oscillation?

Options:

1.       1 Hz

2.       2 Hz

3.       3 Hz

4.       4 Hz

Answer: The correct answer is 1. 1 Hz.

Explanation: The force acting on the block along the incline due to gravity is mgsinθ, where m is the mass of the block, g is the acceleration due to gravity, and θ is the angle of inclination. This force is balanced by the restoring force of the spring, which is k*x, where k is the spring constant and x is the displacement of the block.

In simple harmonic motion, the frequency of oscillation is given by f = (1/2π) * √(k/m). Substituting the given values, we get f = (1/2π) * √(100/2) = 1 Hz.

 

Inertia,

Question: A disc of mass 2 kg and radius 0.1 m is rotating about its axis with an angular velocity of 10 rad/s. If the disc is brought to rest in 5 seconds by applying a constant torque, what is the magnitude of this torque?

Options:

1.       0.04 Nm

2.       0.4 Nm

3.       4 Nm

4.       40 Nm

Answer: The correct answer is 2. 0.4 Nm.

Explanation: The moment of inertia of the disc about its axis is given by I = 0.5mr², where m is the mass of the disc and r is its radius. So, I = 0.52(0.1)² = 0.01 kg*m².

The angular acceleration of the disc is given by α = Δω/Δt, where Δω is the change in angular velocity and Δt is the time taken for this change. So, α = (0 - 10)/5 = -2 rad/s².

The torque required to bring the disc to rest is given by τ = Iα. Substituting the calculated values, we get τ = 0.01(-2) = -0.02 Nm.

However, since torque is a vector quantity and we are interested in its magnitude, we take the absolute value to get τ = 0.02 Nm.

 

Question: A block of mass 5 kg is at rest on a frictionless horizontal surface. A horizontal force F is applied to the block. If the block moves a distance of 10 m in 2 seconds, what is the magnitude of the force F?

Options:

1.       5 N

2.       10 N

3.       20 N

4.       25 N

Answer: The correct answer is 4. 20 N.

Explanation: The acceleration of the block can be calculated using the equation of motion: s = ut + 0.5at², where s is the distance, u is the initial velocity, a is the acceleration, and t is time. Since the block is initially at rest, u = 0, and we get a = 2s/t² = 2*10/4 = 5 m/s².

According to Newton’s second law of motion, the force applied on the block is equal to the mass of the block times its acceleration. So, F = ma = 55 = 25 N

 

Newton’s First law of motion;

 Question: A car of mass 1000 kg is moving with a constant velocity of 20 m/s along a straight road. The driver applies the brakes and brings the car to rest in 10 seconds. What is the magnitude of the net force applied by the brakes?

Options:

1.       1000 N

2.       2000 N

3.       3000 N

4.       4000 N

Answer: The correct answer is 2. 2000 N.

Explanation: According to Newton’s first law of motion, an object in motion continues to move with a constant velocity, unless acted upon by a force. Here, the force is provided by the brakes.

The acceleration of the car can be calculated using the equation a = Δv/Δt, where Δv is the change in velocity and Δt is the time taken for this change. So, a = (0 - 20)/10 = -2 m/s².

According to Newton’s second law of motion, the force applied on the car is equal to the mass of the car times its acceleration. So, F = ma = 1000(-2) = -2000 N.

However, since force is a vector quantity and we are interested in its magnitude, we take the absolute value to get F = 2000 N.

 

Momentum,

 

Question: A ball of mass 0.5 kg is moving with a velocity of 2 m/s. It collides with another stationary ball of mass 1 kg. After the collision, the 0.5 kg ball moves at a velocity of 1 m/s in the same direction. What is the velocity of the 1 kg ball after the collision?

Options:

1.       0.5 m/s

2.       1 m/s

3.       1.5 m/s

4.       2 m/s

Answer: The correct answer is 1. 0.5 m/s.

Explanation: According to the law of conservation of momentum, the total momentum before the collision is equal to the total momentum after the collision.

Before the collision, the total momentum is (0.5 kg * 2 m/s) + (1 kg * 0) = 1 kg*m/s.

After the collision, the total momentum is (0.5 kg * 1 m/s) + (1 kg * v), where v is the velocity of the 1 kg ball.

Setting these two equal gives us (0.5 kg * 1 m/s) + (1 kg * v) = 1 kg*m/s.

Solving for v gives us v = (1 - 0.5) / 1 = 0.5 m/s.

 

Newton’s Second Law of motion,

Question: A force of 20 N is applied to a stationary block of mass 4 kg. The force is applied for a duration of 2 seconds. What is the final velocity of the block?

Options:

1.       5 m/s

2.       10 m/s

3.       15 m/s

4.       20 m/s

Answer: The correct answer is 1. 5 m/s.

Explanation: According to Newton’s second law of motion, the force applied on an object is equal to the change in its momentum (mass times velocity) per unit time. So, the change in velocity (final velocity - initial velocity) is equal to the force applied divided by the mass of the object.

Since the block is initially stationary, its initial velocity is 0. The force applied is 20 N and the mass of the block is 4 kg. So, the final velocity is (20 N / 4 kg) = 5 m/s.

 

Impulses;

Question: A cricket ball of mass 0.15 kg is moving with a velocity of 12 m/s towards a wall. After hitting the wall, the ball rebounds with the same speed but in the opposite direction. If the collision lasts for 0.05 seconds, what is the magnitude of the average force exerted by the wall on the ball?

Options:

1.       36 N

2.       72 N

3.       144 N

4.       288 N

Answer: The correct answer is 3. 144 N.

Explanation: Impulse is defined as the change in momentum of an object when a force is applied over an interval of time. In this case, the initial momentum of the ball is (mass * velocity) = 0.15 kg * 12 m/s = 1.8 kgm/s. The final momentum after rebounding is (mass * velocity) = 0.15 kg * -12 m/s = -1.8 kgm/s.

So, the change in momentum (or impulse) is final momentum - initial momentum = -1.8 kgm/s - 1.8 kgm/s = -3.6 kg*m/s.

The average force exerted by the wall on the ball is then given by impulse / time = -3.6 kg*m/s / 0.05 s = -72 N.

However, since force is a vector quantity and we are interested in its magnitude, we take the absolute value to get F = 72 N.

 

Newton’s Third Law of motion.

Question: A block of mass 2 kg is placed on a frictionless horizontal surface. A force of 10 N is applied to the block, causing it to accelerate. What is the magnitude of the force exerted by the block on the surface?

Options:

1.       0 N

2.       10 N

3.       20 N

4.       30 N

Answer: The correct answer is 2. 10 N.

Explanation: According to Newton’s third law of motion, for every action, there is an equal and opposite reaction. In this case, the action is the force applied to the block, and the reaction is the force exerted by the block on the surface.

Since the force applied to the block is 10 N, the magnitude of the force exerted by the block on the surface must also be 10 N.

 

Law of conservation of linear momentum and its applications.

Question: Two objects of masses 3 kg and 5 kg are moving along the same line in the same direction with velocities of 2 m/s and 1 m/s respectively. They collide and after the collision, the 3 kg object moves with a velocity of 1.5 m/s. What is the velocity of the 5 kg object after the collision?

Options:

1.       0.5 m/s

2.       1 m/s

3.       1.5 m/s

4.       2 m/s

Answer: The correct answer is 1. 0.5 m/s.

Explanation: According to the law of conservation of momentum, the total momentum before the collision is equal to the total momentum after the collision.

Before the collision, the total momentum is (3 kg * 2 m/s) + (5 kg * 1 m/s) = 11 kg*m/s.

After the collision, the total momentum is (3 kg * 1.5 m/s) + (5 kg * v), where v is the velocity of the 5 kg object.

Setting these two equal gives us (3 kg * 1.5 m/s) + (5 kg * v) = 11 kg*m/s.

Solving for v gives us v = (11 - 4.5) / 5 = 0.5 m/s.

 

Equilibrium of concurrent forces.

Question: Three forces F1, F2, and F3 are acting at a point in such a way that the point is in equilibrium. The magnitudes of the forces are F1 = 10 N, F2 = 20 N. The angle between F1 and F2 is 120 degrees. What is the magnitude and direction of the third force F3?

Options:

1.     30 N, opposite to the direction of F1

2.     30 N, opposite to the direction of F2

3.     30 N, in the direction of F1

4.     30 N, in the direction of F2

Answer: The correct answer is 1. 30 N, opposite to the direction of F1.

Explanation: In equilibrium, the vector sum of all forces acting on a point is zero. Therefore, the third force F3 must be such that it cancels out the resultant of F1 and F2.

The resultant R of two forces with magnitudes F1 and F2 acting at an angle θ to each other is given by R = √(F1² + F2² + 2F1F2cosθ). Substituting the given values gives R = √(10² + 20² + 21020cos120) = 30 N.

Since R is the resultant of F1 and F2, the third force F3 must have the same magnitude but act in the opposite direction to R to maintain equilibrium. Since R acts in the same direction as F1 (because F1 < F2), F3 must act in the direction opposite to F1.

 

Static and Kinetic friction,

Question: A block of mass 10 kg is placed on a horizontal surface. The coefficient of static friction between the block and the surface is 0.5. What is the minimum horizontal force required to start moving the block?

Options:

1.     10 N

2.     20 N

3.     30 N

4.     40 N

Answer: The correct answer is 4. 40 N.

Explanation: The force of static friction is given by F = μ * N, where μ is the coefficient of static friction and N is the normal force acting on the object. In this case, the normal force is equal to the weight of the block, which is m * g, where m is the mass of the block and g is the acceleration due to gravity.

Substituting the given values gives F = 0.5 * (10 kg * 9.8 m/s²) = 49 N.

Therefore, a minimum horizontal force of 49 N must be applied to overcome the force of static friction and start moving the block.

Sure, here’s a challenging question on the topic of Kinetic Friction for the JEE Physics examination:

Question: A block of mass 5 kg is sliding down a ramp inclined at an angle of 30 degrees to the horizontal. The coefficient of kinetic friction between the block and the ramp is 0.3. What is the acceleration of the block?

Options:

1.     1 m/s²

2.     2 m/s²

3.     3 m/s²

4.     4 m/s²

Answer: The correct answer is 3. 3 m/s².

Explanation: The force acting on the block along the incline due to gravity is mgsinθ, where m is the mass of the block, g is the acceleration due to gravity, and θ is the angle of inclination. This force is Fg = 5 kg * 9.8 m/s² * sin(30) = 24.5 N.

The force of kinetic friction acting on the block along the incline is μmg*cosθ, where μ is the coefficient of kinetic friction. This force is Ff = 0.3 * 5 kg * 9.8 m/s² * cos(30) = 12.7 N.

The net force acting on the block along the incline is Fg - Ff = 24.5 N - 12.7 N = 11.8 N.

According to Newton’s second law of motion, this net force is equal to the mass of the block times its acceleration (F = m*a). So, a = F/m = 11.8 N / 5 kg = 2.36 m/s², which can be approximated as 2 m/s².

Sure, here’s a challenging question on the topic of Laws of Friction for the JEE Physics examination:

Question: A block of mass 2 kg is placed on a horizontal surface. The coefficient of static friction between the block and the surface is 0.5. A force F is applied horizontally to the block. If the block does not move, what can be said about the magnitude of the force F?

Options:

1.     F must be less than 10 N.

2.     F must be equal to 10 N.

3.     F must be greater than 10 N.

4.     Cannot be determined without more information.

Answer: The correct answer is 1. F must be less than 10 N.

Explanation: The force of static friction that prevents the block from moving is given by F = μ * N, where μ is the coefficient of static friction and N is the normal force acting on the object. In this case, the normal force is equal to the weight of the block, which is m * g, where m is the mass of the block and g is the acceleration due to gravity.

Substituting the given values gives F = 0.5 * (2 kg * 9.8 m/s²) = 9.8 N.

Therefore, as long as the applied force F is less than 9.8 N, it will not overcome the force of static friction and the block will not move.

 

rolling friction.

Sure, here’s a challenging question on the topic of Rolling Friction for the JEE Physics examination:

Question: A solid sphere of mass 5 kg and radius 0.1 m is rolling without slipping on a horizontal surface with a velocity of 2 m/s. The coefficient of rolling friction between the sphere and the surface is 0.02. How far will the sphere travel before it comes to rest?

Options:

1.     10 m

2.     20 m

3.     30 m

4.     40 m

Answer: The correct answer is 2. 20 m.

Explanation: The force of rolling friction acting on the sphere is given by F = μ * N, where μ is the coefficient of rolling friction and N is the normal force acting on the object. In this case, the normal force is equal to the weight of the sphere, which is m * g, where m is the mass of the sphere and g is the acceleration due to gravity.

Substituting the given values gives F = 0.02 * (5 kg * 9.8 m/s²) = 0.98 N.

This force causes a deceleration in the sphere given by a = F/m = 0.98 N / 5 kg = 0.196 m/s².

The distance travelled by the sphere before it comes to rest can be calculated using the equation v² = u² + 2as, where v is the final velocity, u is the initial velocity, a is acceleration (negative in this case), and s is distance.

Rearranging for s gives s = (v² - u²) / (2*a) = (0 - (2 m/s)²) / (2 * -0.196 m/s²) = 20 m.

 

Dynamics of uniform circular motion:

Sure, here’s a challenging question on the topic of Dynamics of Uniform Circular Motion for the JEE Physics examination:

Question: A particle of mass 0.5 kg is moving in a horizontal circle of radius 0.2 m with a constant speed of 5 m/s. What is the magnitude of the net force acting on the particle?

Options:

1.     6.25 N

2.     12.5 N

3.     25 N

4.     50 N

Answer: The correct answer is 1. 6.25 N.

Explanation: In uniform circular motion, the net force acting on the particle is the centripetal force, which is given by F = m*v²/r, where m is the mass of the particle, v is the speed of the particle, and r is the radius of the circular path. Substituting the given values, we get F = 0.5 kg * (5 m/s)² / 0.2 m = 6.25 N.

Options:

1.     400 N

2.     800 N

3.     1600 N

4.     3200 N

Answer: The correct answer is 4. 3200 N.

Explanation: In uniform circular motion, the net force acting on an object is the centripetal force, which is given by F = m*v²/r, where m is the mass of the object, v is the speed of the object, and r is the radius of the circular path. Substituting the given values, we get F = 1000 kg * (20 m/s)² / 50 m = 3200 N.

Sure, here’s another challenging question on the topic of Centrifugal Force for the JEE Physics examination:

Question: A stone of mass 0.5 kg is tied to a string and whirled in a horizontal circle of radius 1 m with a constant speed of 5 m/s. What is the magnitude of the centrifugal force acting on the stone?

Options:

1.     1.25 N

2.     2.5 N

3.     5 N

4.     10 N

Answer: The correct answer is 2. 2.5 N.

Explanation: In uniform circular motion, the centrifugal force experienced by an object is given by F = m*v²/r, where m is the mass of the object, v is the speed of the object, and r is the radius of the circular path. This is essentially the same as centripetal force but acts in the radially outward direction from the center of rotation (as observed in a rotating reference frame). Substituting the given values, we get F = 0.5 kg * (5 m/s)² / 1 m = 2.5 N.
