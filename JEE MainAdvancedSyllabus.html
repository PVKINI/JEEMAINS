2.       20 m/s, East

3.       22.36 m/s, North-East

4.       30 m/s, North-East

The correct answer is option 3. The birds velocity with respect to the ground can be found using vector addition since the velocities are at right angles to each other. The magnitude of the resultant velocity is √((20)^2 + (10)^2) = 22.36 m/s and the direction is North-East.

 

Question: A car travels 100 km to the east, then 100 km to the west. The total time for the journey is 4 hours. What are the average speed and average velocity of the car?

Options:

1.       Speed = 50 km/h, Velocity = 0 km/h

2.       Speed = 50 km/h, Velocity = 50 km/h

3.       Speed = 0 km/h, Velocity = 0 km/h

4.       Speed = 100 km/h, Velocity = 100 km/h

Answer: Option 1.

Explanation: The total distance travelled by the car is 200 km (100 km east + 100 km west), and the time taken is 4 hours. So, the average speed is total distance divided by total time, which is 200 km / 4 h = 50 km/h.

However, because velocity is a vector quantity that takes into account direction, the displacement of the car (final position - initial position) is zero (since it ended up in the same place it started). Therefore, the average velocity is total displacement divided by total time, which is 0 km / 4 h = 0 km/h.

1.       2 m/s

2.       -3 m/s

3.       0 m/s

4.       5 m/s

Answer: Option 2.

Explanation: The velocity of the particle is given by the derivative of the displacement with respect to time. So, v = ds/dt = 3t^2 - 12t + 9. Substituting t = 2 seconds into this equation gives v = 3*(2)^2 - 12*2 + 9 = -3 m/s. Therefore, the velocity of the particle at t = 2 seconds is -3 m/s.

 

MOTION IN A STRAIGHT LINE

Question: A car starts from rest and accelerates uniformly to reach a speed of 21 m/s in 7 seconds. It then continues at this constant speed for 300 meters before applying brakes and stopping uniformly in 10 seconds. What is the total distance covered by the car?

Options:

1.       525 meters

2.       600 meters

3.       675 meters

4.       750 meters

Answer: Option 3.

Explanation: The total distance covered by the car is the sum of the distances covered in each phase of its journey.

1.       During the acceleration phase, the car covers a distance given by the equation of motion s = ut + 0.5at^2, where u is the initial velocity (0 m/s), a is the acceleration (21 m/s / 7 s = 3 m/s^2), and t is the time (7 s). Substituting these values gives s = 07 + 0.53*(7)^2 = 73.5 meters.

2.       The car then travels at a constant speed of 21 m/s for 300 meters, so this phase contributes 300 meters to the total distance.

3.       Finally, during deceleration, the car again covers a distance given by s = ut - 0.5at^2, where u is now the initial velocity (21 m/s), a is the deceleration (21 m/s / 10 s = -2.1 m/s^2), and t is the time (10 s). Substituting these values gives s = 2110 - 0.5(-2.1)*(10)^2 = 301.5 meters.

Adding up these distances gives a total distance of 73.5 + 300 + 301.5 = 675 meters.

Uniform and non-uniform motion

Question: A car travels first half of the distance between two points with a speed of v1 = 30 km/hr and the second half with a speed of v2 = 50 km/hr. What is the average speed of the car for the entire journey?

Options:

1.       37.5 km/hr

2.       40 km/hr

3.       42.5 km/hr

4.       45 km/hr

Answer: Option 2.

Explanation: The average speed for the entire journey is not simply the average of the two speeds, because the time spent at each speed is different. The average speed is given by the total distance divided by the total time. Since the distances traveled at each speed are equal, we can say that the total distance is 2d (where d is either half of the distance). The time to travel each half of the distance is d/v1 and d/v2 respectively. Therefore, the average speed v_avg = 2d / (d/v1 + d/v2) = 2 / (1/v1 + 1/v2). Substituting v1 = 30 km/hr and v2 = 50 km/hr into this equation gives v_avg = 2 / (1/30 + 1/50) = 40 km/hr.

Question: A particle moves along a straight line with an initial velocity of 10 m/s. Its acceleration is given by the equation a = 2t, where t is the time in seconds. What is the velocity of the particle at t = 5 seconds?

Options:

1.       20 m/s

2.       30 m/s

3.       40 m/s

4.       50 m/s

Answer: Option 4.

Explanation: The velocity of the particle at any time t can be found by integrating the acceleration with respect to time. The indefinite integral of a = 2t is v = t^2 + C, where C is the constant of integration. Since the initial velocity of the particle is 10 m/s, we can find C by setting t = 0 and v = 10 in the equation v = t^2 + C, which gives C = 10. Therefore, the equation for the velocity of the particle is v = t^2 + 10. Substituting t = 5 seconds into this equation gives v = (5)^2 + 10 = 50 m/s.

2.       Non-uniform motion with constant acceleration

3.       Non-uniform motion with non-constant acceleration

Furthermore, the acceleration of the particle is given by the rate of change of its velocity. Since the velocity is changing continuously, the acceleration must also be changing continuously. Therefore, the particle is undergoing non-uniform motion with non-constant acceleration.

Momentum,

 

Question: A ball of mass 0.5 kg is moving with a velocity of 2 m/s. It collides with another stationary ball of mass 1 kg. After the collision, the 0.5 kg ball moves at a velocity of 1 m/s in the same direction. What is the velocity of the 1 kg ball after the collision?

Options:

1.       0.5 m/s

2.       1 m/s

3.       1.5 m/s

4.       2 m/s

Answer: The correct answer is 1. 0.5 m/s.

Explanation: According to the law of conservation of momentum, the total momentum before the collision is equal to the total momentum after the collision.

Before the collision, the total momentum is (0.5 kg * 2 m/s) + (1 kg * 0) = 1 kg*m/s.

After the collision, the total momentum is (0.5 kg * 1 m/s) + (1 kg * v), where v is the velocity of the 1 kg ball.

Setting these two equal gives us (0.5 kg * 1 m/s) + (1 kg * v) = 1 kg*m/s.

Solving for v gives us v = (1 - 0.5) / 1 = 0.5 m/s.

2.     VA = -2 m/s, VB = 4 m/s

3.     VA = 0 m/s, VB = 4 m/s

4.     VA = 4 m/s, VB = 2 m/s

Answer: The correct answer is 1. VA = 2 m/s, VB = 3 m/s.

The initial kinetic energy of the system is KE_initial = 0.5 * mA * (VA_initial) + 0.5 * mB * (VB_initial) = 0.5 * 1 kg * (4 m/s) + 0.5 * 2 kg * (2 m/s) = 12 J.

Lets denote the final velocities of A and B as VA_final and VB_final respectively.

From conservation of momentum, we have P_final = mA * VA_final + mB * VB_final. Substituting P_final = P_initial, we get 1 kg * VA_final + 2 kg * VB_final = 8 kg*m/s (Equation 1).

From conservation of kinetic energy, we have KE_final = KE_initial, which gives us 0.5 * mA * (VA_final) + 0.5 * mB * (VB_final) = 12 J (Equation 2).

Solving Equations (1) and (2) simultaneously, we get VA_final = 2 m/s and VB_final = 3 m/s.

If the length of the wire is doubled, L becomes 2L. However, since the applied force and the material of the wire are kept constant, the stress in the wire remains the same. Therefore, the strain in the wire (which is equal to Stress/Y) also remains the same.

Since strain is defined as the extension divided by the original length, and both the strain and original length have doubled, it follows that the extension ΔL' must remain constant.

Hence, the new extension in the wire is ΔL.

Question: A solid cube of side a and volume V is subjected to a uniform pressure P that causes a decrease in its volume ΔV. The material of the cube has a bulk modulus B. If the pressure applied is doubled, what will be the new change in volume?

Options:

1.     ΔV

3.     ΔV/2

Answer: The correct answer is Option 2: 2ΔV.

Explanation: According to the definition of bulk modulus, B = P/(ΔV/V), where P is the applied pressure.

If the pressure applied is doubled, P becomes 2P. Substituting this into the formula, we get B = 2P/(ΔV'/V), where ΔV' is the new change in volume.

Comparing this with the original formula, we find that ΔV' = 2ΔV.

Hence, the new change in volume is 2ΔV.

Sure, heres a challenging question on Modulus of Rigidity for your JEE preparation:

Question: A solid cylinder of radius r and length L is subjected to a shearing force F that causes a lateral displacement x in its top surface. The material of the cylinder has a modulus of rigidity η. If the shearing force applied is doubled, what will be the new lateral displacement?

Options:

1.     x

2.     2x

3.     x/2

4.     4x

Answer: The correct answer is Option 2: 2x.

Explanation: According to the definition of modulus of rigidity, η = F/(A*x), where F is the applied shearing force, A is the area over which the force is applied (which is equal to πr for a cylinder), and x is the lateral displacement.

If the shearing force applied is doubled, F becomes 2F. Substituting this into the formula, we get η = 2F/(A*x'), where x' is the new lateral displacement.

Comparing this with the original formula, we find that x' = 2x.

Hence, the new lateral displacement is 2x.

Sure, heres a challenging question on Pressure Due to Fluid Column for your JEE preparation:

Question: A fluid column of height h and density ρ exerts a pressure P at its base. The acceleration due to gravity is g. If the height of the fluid column is doubled and its density is halved, what will be the new pressure at the base of the fluid column?

Options:

1.     P

2.     2P

3.     P/2

4.     4P

Answer: The correct answer is Option 1: P.

Explanation: The pressure P at the base of a fluid column is given by the formula P = ρgh, where ρ is the density of the fluid, g is the acceleration due to gravity, and h is the height of the fluid column.

If the height of the fluid column is doubled, h becomes 2h. If the density of the fluid is halved, ρ becomes ρ/2.

Substituting these values into the formula, we get P' = (ρ/2)g(2h) = ρgh = P.

Hence, the new pressure at the base of the fluid column is P.
